# K Academy — Student Funding Strategy

How to get student funding approved for K Academy, and how it is presented on
the website so students can apply. Owner brief: secure grants/loans for students
into the academy, using our UKRLP registration (UKPRN 10101305) and, where
possible, Islington Council support.

Status: working plan, June 2026. Owner-chosen priority: apply for our **own ESFA
Advanced Learner Loans facility** (Level 3 and Level 4) first.

---

## 1. The short version

- **Our courses can be funded.** Government funding only flows to Ofqual-regulated
  qualifications. Our Level 2, 3 and 4 courses are Ofqual-regulated VTCT
  qualifications, so they clear the gate. Our Level 5–7 courses are currently
  CPD-only and **cannot** be government-funded as they stand.
- **The UKPRN is the entry ticket, not the funding.** Being on the UK Register of
  Learning Providers (UKPRN 10101305) is required to draw down most funding, but
  it grants nothing on its own. Each route below needs its own approval.
- **Five routes, two of them live today.** Monthly course finance and self/employer
  funding work now. Advanced Learner Loans, the Mayor of London Adult Skills Fund,
  Islington Council learning, and (from 2027) the Lifelong Learning Entitlement all
  need an approval we do not yet hold.
- **Priority application: our own Advanced Learner Loans facility** for the Level 3
  and Level 4 courses. This is the most direct government route for a private
  provider and fits our regulated catalogue.
- **The website is already capturing demand.** The new `/academy/funding` page lets
  students check eligibility and apply; every enquiry lands in
  `/admin/academy/funding`. That pipeline of real, named demand is also the
  evidence the funder applications need.

---

## 2. What the UKPRN gives us (and what it doesn't)

UKPRN 10101305 means K Academy is on the **UK Register of Learning Providers
(UKRLP)**, operated for the Department for Education. The register only confirms
the organisation exists — it carries no quality endorsement and no funding.

It is, however, a precondition: you cannot hold an ESFA/DfE funding agreement, a
Student Loans Company provider account, a GLA Adult Skills Fund contract, or an
Office for Students registration without one. So we have step zero done. Every
route below is a separate approval layered on top of the UKPRN.

---

## 3. The eligibility gate: regulated qualifications

This is the single most important fact in this document.

Mainstream government funding (Advanced Learner Loans, the Adult Skills Fund, the
Lifelong Learning Entitlement) pays only for **regulated qualifications on the
relevant approved funding list** — identified by their Ofqual reference / QAN.

| Course | Level | Accreditation | Fundable in principle? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foundation in Skin & Laser | 2 | Ofqual-regulated VTCT | Yes — via Adult Skills Fund (London) |
| Certificate in Laser & Aesthetic Therapies | 3 | Ofqual-regulated VTCT | Yes — Advanced Learner Loan and/or Adult Skills Fund |
| Certificate in Aesthetic Practice | 4 | Ofqual-regulated VTCT | Yes — Advanced Learner Loan (later LLE) |
| Advanced Aesthetics | 5–7 | CPD only | No (not until mapped to a regulated qualification) |

**Action — verify the QANs.** Being VTCT-regulated is necessary but not
sufficient: each specific qualification must appear on the funding list for the
route. Before any application, confirm with VTCT and on the DfE "find a learning
aim" service that our exact Level 3 and Level 4 QANs are approved for Advanced
Learner Loans, and that the Level 2/3 QANs are approved for the Adult Skills
Fund. If a course we deliver is not on the list, ask VTCT which equivalent
regulated qualification is, and switch the funded cohorts to it.

**Strategic note on Level 5–7.** To make the advanced pathway fundable later,
either (a) deliver it as a regulated qualification (e.g. an Ofqual-regulated
Level 7 in clinical aesthetic practice through an awarding organisation), or (b)
target it for the Lifelong Learning Entitlement from 2027 (Levels 4–6). CPD
certificates will not attract public funding.

## 4. Route A — Advanced Learner Loans (PRIORITY)

**What it is.** A government loan that pays the learner's course fee for
Ofqual-regulated qualifications at **Levels 3 to 6**, for learners aged **19+**.
It is not means-tested. The learner repays only once they earn above the income
threshold, through the tax system, and any balance is written off after the set
period. For the learner it behaves like a student loan; for us, the Student Loans
Company pays the fee **directly to the provider** on the learner's behalf.

**Why it's our first application.** It maps onto our regulated Level 3 and Level 4
courses, it does not depend on a London procurement window, and it puts us in
direct control as the funded provider.

**What we must do to offer it.** The shape of the process (confirm current-year
detail with ESFA/DfE, as forms change each year):

1. **Hold the UKPRN.** Done (10101305).
2. **Confirm fundable qualifications.** Check our Level 3 and Level 4 QANs are on
   the Advanced Learner Loans qualification list (section 3).
3. **Watch for a facility window.** New first-time loan facilities are awarded
   through ESFA selection exercises announced in the ESFA update/newsletter.
   Register for ESFA updates so we catch the window.
4. **Submit the first-time facility request.** Complete the ESFA "advanced learner
   loans facility: first-time facility request" for the funding year. ESFA assess
   capacity, financial health and quality before awarding a facility (a capped
   value we can draw down) and, if requested, a **loans bursary** allocation.
5. **Sign the ESFA loans agreement** and complete due diligence (financial health
   check, policies, safeguarding, data protection, subcontracting declaration if
   relevant).
6. **Register with the Student Loans Company's Learning Provider Services** so SLC
   can pay us on behalf of learners, and so learners can apply for their loan
   against our courses.
7. **Set up delivery and compliance.** Individualised Learner Record (ILR) returns
   via the DfE "Submit Learner Data" service, evidence of learning, attendance,
   and learner eligibility checks. Expect to be in scope for **Ofsted** as a new
   funded provider (a new-provider monitoring visit, typically within the first
   couple of years).

**Loans bursary fund.** Providers with a facility also receive a bursary fund to
help learners with hardship, childcare, travel and learning support. Offering
this strengthens recruitment from lower-income learners.

**Timeline.** Plan for several months: a facility window, the application and due
diligence, SLC onboarding, then learner applications. Treat it as a 6–12 month
runway to first funded enrolments, not weeks.

**Honesty/advertising rule.** Until the facility is approved we must **not**
advertise that students can get an Advanced Learner Loan through us. The website
currently presents this route as "register interest" only (section 12). Flip it to
"available" only once the agreement is signed.

**Note on the future.** Advanced Learner Loans are being wound into the Lifelong
Learning Entitlement (section 7). Level 4–6 loan funding for providers **not**
registered with the Office for Students is confirmed to continue (extended) from
2025–26 through to 2029–30, so this route has a clear runway even as LLE starts.

## 5. Route B — Adult Skills Fund (Mayor of London)

**What it is.** Adult education funding for learners aged 19+, devolved from
central government to the **Mayor of London / Greater London Authority** for
London residents (it was the Adult Education Budget; renamed the Adult Skills Fund
in 2024). Because the clinic and most learners are in London, **this is the route
for our London learners, not the national ESFA one.** Eligible Londoners can train
**free or at reduced cost** on qualifying courses, with priority for people who are
unemployed or on a low wage (the GLA sets a low-wage threshold — currently around
£25,000 a year; confirm the current figure, and note Islington applies its own
local threshold, quoted around £28,860).

**The catch for new providers.** GLA grant allocations are largely **lagged**
(this year's delivery sets next year's allocation), and the grant-funded list is
close to full (~71 providers in 2025–26). New entrants get in by either:

- **Subcontracting** with an existing GLA-funded college or provider — fastest
  start, lower compliance overhead, the funded provider holds the contract and
  passes work (and a management fee) to us; or
- **Procurement** — bidding when the GLA runs a competitive tender for new
  provision (watch london.gov.uk decisions and the GLA e-tendering portal).

**Recommended start:** approach two or three GLA-funded London colleges about
subcontracting our regulated Level 2/3 delivery to eligible residents, in parallel
with the loans facility work. Subcontracting gets funded learners through the door
while we build our own track record.

**Eligibility (typical).** 19+, lived in the UK/EEA for 3+ years or settled
status, London resident, and the qualification on the GLA funding list. Full
funding for unemployed/low-wage learners; co-funding otherwise.

## 6. Route C — Islington Council

Islington runs **Islington Adult Community Learning (IACL)**, sitting in the
council's Community Wealth Building / Inclusive Economy & Jobs directorate. It
offers free and subsidised courses to residents aged 19+ who are unemployed or on
a low income (it advertises eligibility around earning under ~£28,860). This is
itself adult-skills-funded delivery, plus the council's own community learning and
employment-support programmes.

**How to use it.**

1. **Partnership / subcontracting with IACL** — offer our regulated entry-level
   provision (Level 2/3 Skin & Laser) as a route into a regulated career for
   Islington residents. The community-wealth-building agenda (local jobs, local
   training, local clinic) is a strong fit; lead with that.
2. **UK Shared Prosperity Fund (UKSPF) "People & Skills"** — Islington administers
   UKSPF locally and can fund training that moves residents toward work. Ask the
   council's employment/skills team whether current UKSPF activity can commission
   places on our courses. (UKSPF is time-limited — confirm the current funding
   window.)
3. **Local employer/skills initiatives** — the council and Islington GP Federation
   run training hubs; a clinic-based aesthetics academy that hires locally is a
   natural partner.

**First step:** a single approach to Islington's Adult Community Learning / Inclusive
Economy team proposing (a) funded places for residents and (b) a local-jobs
pipeline from training into clinic roles.

## 7. Route D — Lifelong Learning Entitlement (from 2027, strategic)

**What it is.** From **January 2027** (applications open September 2026) the
Lifelong Learning Entitlement replaces Advanced Learner Loans for most higher
study. Every adult gets a flexible loan allowance worth about **£39,160** (four
years of study) to spend across their life on **Level 4 to 6** courses **and
individual modules** (modules of at least 30 credits).

**What it requires of us.** To register students for LLE funding, a provider must
be **registered with the Office for Students (OfS)** under the "approved" or
"approved (fee cap)" category. OfS registration is a significant undertaking
(quality, financial sustainability, management and governance conditions) and is
the highest bar of any route here.

**Our position.** This is the **strategic prize** for the advanced (Level 4–6)
pathway and modular short courses, but it is a 2027+ project. The pragmatic
sequence is: run Level 3/4 on Advanced Learner Loans now (extended to 2029–30 for
non-OfS providers), and pursue OfS registration in parallel so we can move the
higher-level and modular offer onto LLE when it lands.

## 8. Route E — Private course finance (live today)

The route students can use **immediately**, and the one most aesthetics academies
actually run. The learner spreads the fee over monthly instalments; we are paid up
front by the finance provider.

**Current state.** The site offers **Clearpay**, which caps near £1,000 — too low
for a £3,500 Level 4 course. We need a **course-finance partner** built for
training fees.

**Options (provider-agnostic — pick on commercial terms):**

| Provider | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| **Payl8r** | Widely used by UK aesthetics academies; handles higher course values and longer terms. |
| **Tabeo** | Common in clinics/dentistry; strong for higher-value plans. |
| **Snap Finance / Knuckle / Deko / Omni Capital** | Point-of-sale instalment lenders; terms vary. |
| **Klarna / Clearpay** | Keep for small amounts only; not suited to £2k–£3.5k courses. |

**Compliance.** Offering or brokering credit is regulated. Either the partner is
the lender and we operate within an introducer/credit-broker permission, or we use
a provider model that keeps us out of FCA scope — confirm with the chosen partner
and, if needed, our compliance adviser. Financial promotions on the site must be
fair, clear and not misleading.

**Website integration (built — section 12).** The funding page presents monthly
finance as available now, with a worked example and an "Apply with monthly
finance" link that points to the partner's hosted application once configured.

## 9. Other levers worth a look

- **Free Courses for Jobs (Level 3 adult offer).** Within the Adult Skills Fund, a
  first full Level 3 can be free for eligible adults. If our Level 3 QAN is on the
  list, this funds it through the GLA route.
- **Skills Bootcamps.** Flexible Level 3–5 training (up to ~16 weeks) with a job
  outcome, funded via the GLA in London. A clinic-based "route into aesthetics"
  bootcamp could be commissioned — worth exploring with the GLA.
- **Learner support / hardship and bursary funds.** Come with the loans facility
  and ASF; help lower-income learners with travel, childcare and materials.
- **Employer sponsorship.** Many clinics/salons pay for staff training. We can
  invoice employers directly — already presented as a route on the site.
- **Equipment leasing for graduates.** Already offered; keep it in the funding
  story as "how you fund the practice after you qualify."
- **Discontinued — do not pursue:** Professional and Career Development Loans
  (closed 2019).

## 11. Compliance and risk

- **Do not advertise unapproved funding.** Only present a route as available once
  the agreement is in place. The site enforces this with per-route status
  (`available` vs `register interest`).
- **Consumer credit.** Monthly finance is regulated; operate within the partner's
  model and the correct FCA permissions; keep financial promotions fair and clear.
- **Funding audit.** ESFA/GLA funding brings audit, evidence-of-learning and
  eligibility-checking duties, and likely Ofsted inspection. Budget for the
  compliance overhead, not just the income.
- **Data protection.** Funding enquiries capture special-category-adjacent data
  (employment, income band, residency). Stored against the K Clinics tenant with
  staff-only access; keep it inside existing CRM retention and privacy policies,
  and reference funding data in the privacy notice.

## 12. What is already built on the website (this change)

So students can apply for funding through the site today, this change adds:

- **Public page `/academy/funding`** — explains the five routes with honest
  "available now" vs "register interest" status, a six-question **eligibility
  self-check** that recommends routes, a **funding application form**, a
  monthly-finance worked example, and a funding FAQ.
- **Application capture** — `POST /api/academy/funding` stores each enquiry
  (`FundingApplication` model) with the eligibility snapshot and emails the team +
  the applicant. Honeypot + rate-limited like the existing apply flow.
- **Admin `/admin/academy/funding`** — staff list of enquiries with a status
  pipeline (New → Reviewing → Referred → Approved → Declined → Funded → Closed),
  notes, and the applicant's self-check answers. A "Funding" card on the
  `/admin/academy` hub shows new-enquiry count.
- **Promotion** — funding CTAs on the academy landing page and each course page,
  plus an "Academy Funding" link in the site footer.
- **Provider-agnostic finance** — routes/config live in `lib/funding.ts`. Set
  `COURSE_FINANCE_URL` to the chosen partner's hosted application link to switch the
  monthly-finance CTA on; no code change needed to pick a partner.

**Go-live checklist for the page**
1. Pick the course-finance partner; set `COURSE_FINANCE_URL` / `COURSE_FINANCE_NAME`.
2. Point `ACADEMY_NOTIFY_EMAIL` at the inbox that should receive funding enquiries.
3. Leave government routes as "register interest" until each approval lands, then
   change the route's `status` to `available` in `lib/funding.ts`.
4. Add funding data to the privacy notice.
