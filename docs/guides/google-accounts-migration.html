Moving Google Analytics & Google Ads into your K Clinics Workspace

For the account owner · ~15 minutes · You need a computer (not a phone)

Your website statistics (Google Analytics) and your advertising account (Google Ads) are still owned by your old personal Google login — the one that first set them up. Your new K Clinics Workspace login (for example owner@kclinics.co.uk) has no access yet, so the website cannot read your traffic and the Analytics page shows an error.

Good news: nothing gets moved or deleted — all your history stays exactly where it is. You simply give your new login permission to the two accounts, then connect it once inside the K Clinics admin. Follow the parts in order.

Have these ready before you start:
1. Your OLD Google login (the one that owns Analytics & Ads).
2. Your NEW K Clinics login, e.g. owner@kclinics.co.uk. Write it down — you'll type it a few times.
3. Both passwords. Do this at a desktop/laptop.

Part 1 · Give your new login access to Google Analytics

  1. Open a web browser and go to analytics.google.com. Sign in with your OLD Google login (the one that owns Analytics).
  2. At the very bottom-left of the screen, click the gear icon ⚙ Admin.
  3. You'll now see two columns. If you have more than one website listed, click the property dropdown at the top and choose your K Clinics website property first. Then, in the right-hand column headed Property, click Access management.
  4. At the top-right of the list, click the blue ＋ button, then click Add users.
  5. In the Email addresses box, type your NEW K Clinics login exactly — e.g. owner@kclinics.co.uk.
  6. Leave Notify new users by email ticked.
  7. Under Direct roles and data restrictions, choose Administrator. Leave the two data-restriction boxes unticked.
  8. At the top-right, click Add.
Done when: your new email appears in the Property access management list with the role Administrator.

Now copy your Property ID (you'll need it in Part 4)

  1. Still in ⚙ Admin, in the Property column click Property details (older menus call it Property settings).
  2. Near the top you'll see PROPERTY ID — a number such as 123456789. This is NOT the G-XXXXXXX tag. Copy the number and keep it for Part 4.

Part 2 · Give your new login access to Google Ads

  1. Go to ads.google.com. Sign in with your OLD Google login (the one that owns the ad account).
  2. In the left-hand menu, click Admin (near the bottom). Then click Access and security.
  3. Make sure the Users tab is selected (top of the table).
  4. Click the blue ＋ button above the table.
  5. In the Email box, type your NEW K Clinics login exactly — e.g. owner@kclinics.co.uk.
  6. For Select an access level, choose Admin.
  7. Click Send invitation.
Done when: your new email shows in the Users list with the status “Invitation sent / pending.”

Part 3 · Accept the invitations from your new login

  1. Open a fresh browser window (or an Incognito/Private window so it doesn't reuse the old login).
  2. Google Analytics: usually nothing to accept — access is granted immediately. The “Notify” email is just a heads-up.
  3. Google Ads: open the inbox of your NEW login (e.g. owner@kclinics.co.uk). Find the email titled “You’ve been invited to access a Google Ads account.” Click Accept invitation, sign in with the new login if asked, and confirm.
Done when: signed in as your new login, you can see the ad account at ads.google.com and the website property at analytics.google.com.

Part 4 · Connect it inside the K Clinics admin

  1. Sign in to the K Clinics admin at kclinics.co.uk/admin as usual.
  2. In the left menu open Marketing → Connections.
  3. On the Google row, click Reconnect (it may say Connect).
  4. A Google sign-in window opens. This is the important bit: choose your NEW K Clinics login (owner@kclinics.co.uk) — not the old one. If it shows the old account, click Use another account and sign in with the new one.
  5. On the blue permissions screen, leave every box ticked and click Continue (or Allow).
  6. Now set the property number: open Settings → Credentials. Find GA4 property ID (numeric) and paste the number you copied in Part 1. Click Save.
Done when: open Marketing → Website analytics and reload — your visitor numbers appear instead of the red error banner.

If something doesn’t work

What you seeWhat it means & what to do
“You don’t have permission to add users”You’re signed in with the wrong account. Sign in with the original owner login that created Analytics / Ads, then retry that part.
The analytics page still shows the red 401 / “returned an error” banner after connectingIn Part 4 you must pick the new login in the Google window, and that login must have at least Viewer on the property (Part 1). Click Reconnect again and choose the new account.
Numbers look wrong / from the wrong siteThe Property ID in Part 4 must be the number of the property that holds this website’s data. Re-check it in Analytics → ⚙ Admin → Property details.
You can’t find the invitation email (Ads)Check spam, and confirm you typed the new email correctly in Part 2. You can re-send from the same Users list.

Optional · Full ownership later

The steps above share the accounts with your K Clinics login, which is all the website needs and keeps every bit of history. If you later want the accounts fully owned by K Clinics (for billing and long-term control), that’s a separate, heavier process — transferring ownership/billing — best done once everything above is working.
